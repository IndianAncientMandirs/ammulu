Buddhist Monuments at Sanchi

The Buddhist Monuments at Sanchi, located in Madhya Pradesh, are among the oldest and most significant Buddhist heritage sites in India. Dating back to the 3rd century BCE, these monuments were originally commissioned by Emperor Ashoka of the Maurya dynasty, making them a cornerstone of Buddhist history. The Great Stupa, the most famous structure at Sanchi, is a massive hemispherical dome housing sacred relics of Lord Buddha.
